First Event

Master Mobile UX

  • Lesson 1: Step 1

    1. Designing for the smallest thing first makes beautiful content.
    2. The iPhone brought a paradigm shift. This paradigm shift skyrocketed in 2011. This happened due to desktops was used by each individual in the house and would be replaced every 4-5 years. Mobile devices however are different in that each person in the home may have one and they are replaced on average at least once every two years.
    3. Whatever you are doing, do mobile first -Eric Schmidt
    4. Just because your device is responsive does not mean it's mobile first.
    5. Mobile first is time consuming and costly for a rebuild.
      But this provides the best user experience.

      6. Rewiring your brain

      1. Desktop computer sales have been declining since 2011
      2. You don't see people with their heads in laptops
      3. Start small and scale up
      4. Mobile is now a fad.

    Lesson 1: Step 2

    1. A lot of times, people don't know what they want until you show them - Steve Jobs
    2. Be There.
    3. Be Useful.
    4. Be Quick.
    5. Reachability Matters. How our users handle their device.
    6. 1/2 second delay = 20% drop in traffic - Google
    7. Networks Matter.
      Not everybody has the latest and greatest
    8. Reduce image usage
    9. SVG/Webfonts are lower bandwidth
    10. Optimize and minify CSS and JS files
    11. GZIP files to eliminate unnecessary data

    Lesson 1: Step 3

    1. Pay attention to the way users use their devices.
    2. Primary Controls on the bottom is actually a good design space.

    Lesson 1: Step 4

    1. Reduce Cognitive load.
    2. Reduce the number of fields, users don't want to scroll.
    3. You can use JavaScript to make forms easier to use.
    4. For large forms, use pagination and a progress indicator.
    5. Autofill will help users to complete a form.
    6. Pay attention to the keyboard being used.

    Lesson 1: Step 5

    1. End users, not technology shape the market. Matt - Haig
    2. Before you advertise, research.
    3. The are more advertisements on a screen on average than actual content.
    4. Popups are detested.
    5. Mobile ads are still designed for desktop.
    6. Stop using popups and pop-overs.
    7. Users didn't come to your site to look at ads.
    8. Keep it simple and sleek.
    9. Make sure it's unobtrusive. Content First.
